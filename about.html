About the Founder

Matthew Korpman

I'm a biblical scholar, theology professor, and pastor who spent years frustrated with how theology is taught and practiced in the church.

Seminary gave me tools. But years later, standing in front of congregations with hard questions about the Bible, I realized those tools were out of date. Biblical scholarship had moved forward. The church hadn't. And most pastors felt as isolated and behind as I did.

So I built TheoEducation. Not to give you answers, but to give you the same frameworks I use—the ones biblical scholars use—so you can ask better questions and lead with integrity.

The Background

I grew up in the Seventh-day Adventist church, pursued biblical studies obsessively, and earned multiple degrees trying to understand Scripture deeply.

But sitting in seminary classrooms, I noticed something: the scholars weren't teaching pastors. We existed in separate worlds. Pastors used old frameworks. Scholars published findings no one in the pulpit ever saw.

The gap felt criminal. Congregants deserved better teaching. Pastors deserved access to current scholarship. And honest questions about the Bible shouldn't be treated like threats.

That's when I decided: I'd become the bridge. I'd translate scholarship into something pastors could actually use.
