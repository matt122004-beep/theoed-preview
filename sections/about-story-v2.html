The Journey Began in Isolation

As an adjunct professor of biblical studies, Matthew spent years in intellectual isolation. He held a Master of Arts in Religion from Yale Divinity School and four undergraduate degrees spanning theology, archaeology, philosophy, and religious studies—but he was teaching part-time, leading church services on weekends, and watching pastors struggle with the same hard theological questions he'd spent years studying.

The disconnect was clear: pastors wanted rigor. They wanted scholarship. They wanted to answer the questions their congregations were already asking about the historical Jesus, biblical authority, and textual criticism. But high-quality theological education wasn't accessible. Seminary was expensive, geographically limited, and took years.

So Matthew did what scholars do: he built a platform to share what he'd learned.
